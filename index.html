Do You Have A Website?

Cynthia Kiser, cnk@caltech.edu

Outline for this Session

  • Do you need a website?
  • What is the purpose of your website?
  • What should your site say?
  • How to build your site using Caltech Sites

Do you need a website?

Maybe. (probably). But what about alternatives?

  • LinkedIn
  • Specific networking sites, e.g. Mendeley

Those sites have eyeballs, which is good. But they do not pull together everything about you and / or let you showcase your story the way you want to tell it.

What is the purpose of your website?

That depends on your goals.

  • Get an academic job
  • Get an industry job
  • Teach / outreach

Before you start building your site, think about your audience(s). Are you writing for present and future collegues? Do you also want to write for a more general audience? kids? policy makers? your family?

Personal stuff probably belongs somewhere else: a personal web site or a collection of Instagram, Facebook, etc. pages.

What should your site say?

Look at examples of people who are where you want to be - and emulate them.

Start basic:

  • Your contact information
  • What is your current research?
  • And why should we care about it?

What should your site say? (2)

Time permitting add:

  • Previous research
  • Future directions
  • Teaching experience / teaching statement

Optional extras:

  • Videos of you speaking
  • Speaker slide decks (but beware of publication embargos)
  • Blog

How to build your site using Caltech Sites

Prep

  • Decide your domain name, e,g, cynthiakiser.people.caltech.edu
  • Request a site
  • Find some images - perhaps a headshot or diagrams from your publications
  • Inspiration:
    • http://qubit.caltech.edu (especially the publications page)
    • http://sarkis.caltech.edu
    • http://rubino.people.caltech.edu

Steps

  • Sketch possible home page layouts
  • Log into your new site & create first version of your home page
  • Optional: create secondary pages
