Do you need a website?

Maybe. (probably). But what about alternatives?

  • LinkedIn
  • Specific networking sites, e.g. Mendeley

Those sites have eyeballs (good) but they do not pull together everything about you and / or let you showcase your story the way you want to tell it.

What is the purpose of your website?

That depends on your goals.

  • Get an academic job
  • Get an industry job
  • Teach / outreach

Before you start building your site, think about your audience(s). Are you writing for present and future collegues? Do you also want to write for a more general audience? kids? policy makers? your family?

Personal stuff probably belongs somewhere else: a personal web site or a collection of Instagram, Facebook, etc. pages.

What should your site say?

Look at examples of people who are where you want to be - and emulate them.

Start basic: What is your current research? And why should we care about it? + contact information

Time permitting add to this. What previous research experience have you had? and how has that contributed to your future research direction?

Optional extras:

  • Videos of you speaking
  • Speaker slide decks (careful republication embargos)
  • Blog
